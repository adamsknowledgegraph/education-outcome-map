# Project Brief

## Problem

Students cannot easily see how degrees translate into careers, and what those careers imply for actual lifestyle.

Current education and career tools usually show:

- degree descriptions
- salary ranges
- vague career options

They rarely show:

- practical quality-of-life implications
- hourly earning power
- likely paths from degree to profession
- tradeoffs between interests, effort, and lifestyle

## Product Vision

Build an interactive experience where users can move between:

- degrees
- professions
- skills
- income outcomes
- lifestyle scenarios

## V1 Geography

US-only for the first version.

Why:

- best public data availability
- easier normalization
- direct hourly wage data
- faster MVP path

## Most Important Insight

`$/hour` is more intuitive than just annual salary.

Why:

- it helps users imagine daily life
- it makes job comparisons feel more real
- it can connect directly to lifestyle assumptions

## Core Experience

### Path Explorer

Degree -> profession -> skills network

### Lifestyle Explorer

Users pick:

- city or cost level
- simple / comfortable / luxury taste
- work-life preference
- interests

Then the dashboard shows:

- paths that fit those constraints
- likely earnings
- likely lifestyle fit
- gaps between desired and expected outcome

### Compare View

Compare 2-4 degree paths side by side.

## Candidate Data Inputs

- university program catalogs
- occupation and employment statistics
- wage or salary datasets
- alumni or career outcome data
- cost-of-living and lifestyle assumptions
- skills taxonomies

For V1, these should be US datasets first.

## MVP Questions To Answer

- What degrees most often lead to which careers?
- What do those careers pay per year and per hour?
- What skills tend to matter along the way?
- What kind of lifestyle can that realistically support?
- Which paths best match a user’s interests and desired life?

## Success Criteria

- understandable to non-experts
- visually compelling
- data-backed
- useful enough to share
- easy to extend with better datasets later
